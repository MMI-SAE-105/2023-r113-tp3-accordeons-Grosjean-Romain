Earth

The third planet from the sun, Earth is the only place in the known universe confirmed to harbour life. With a radius of 5,000 km, the Earth is the fifth largest planet in our solar system, and the only one that is certain to have liquid water on its surface. Sources : Wikipedia and National Geographic.

Mars

Mars is a telluric planet, i.e. rocky. ten times less massive than Earth. View of the Blue Planet, it appears reddish due to the iron oxide-rich dust that covers its surface. Once hydrated the iron oxide becomes rusty, hence the ochre colour of our neighbouring planet. Sources : Wikipedia and Stelvision.

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun. After Jupiter, it is the largest planet in the Solar System. Like all the other planets, Saturn probably formed in a large cloud of gas, dust and ice. This cloud flattened out into a rotating disc. Sources : Wikipedia and the Canadian Space Agency.
