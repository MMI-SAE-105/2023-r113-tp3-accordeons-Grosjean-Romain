Pangea

At the beginning of the 20th century, Alfred Wegener noticed that the layout of the continents meant that the east coast of South America seemed to fit perfectly into the west coast of Africa. Others had realised this long before him, but he was the first to use this observation to propose the theory of continental drift: a supercontinent, Pangea, broke up at the beginning of the Secondary Era and, since then, the continental masses resulting from this fragmentation have drifted along the Earth's surface.
